Lesson Overview: Dividing Fractions

The Michigan Department of Education (n.d.) states on page 44 that the focus of this lesson is to apply and extend previous understandings of multiplication and division to divide fractions by fractions. Students will work toward interpreting and computing quotients of fractions and solving word problems using visual models and equations.

Instructional Approach: The Power of Questioning

As noted by Dance and Kaplan (2018) in Chapter 5, it is essential to encourage students to think deeply as they read, questioning an author’s reasoning and motivations, and to apply that same level of inquiry to mathematics. To guide students through the complexities of dividing fractions, the teacher will employ three specific categories of questioning:

1. Questions that Clarify and Probe Thinking

To ensure students understand the context of a word problem—such as how many 3/4-cup servings are in 2/3 of a cup of yogurt—Dance and Kaplan (2018) explain in Chapter 5 that clarification questions help students visualize the context of the problem.

Example Question: "What do we know about the amount of yogurt we are starting with?"

Probing Question: "Can you explain what a 'serving' represents in this situation?"

2. Questions that Guide, Challenge, and Extend

As Dance and Kaplan (2018) describe in Chapter 5, these questions create cognitive dissonance to increase learning. In this lesson, they will be used to help students connect the relationship between multiplication and division.

Example Question: "Why did you choose to use a visual area model to represent 2/3?"

Extension Question: "How does your model prove that 3/4 of 8/9 is actually 2/3?"
